The patterns are so old I don't recognize them as patterns anymore. They're just... how I am. I give until I'm empty, then I give more. I say yes when I mean no because the word "no" feels like a door slamming and I've heard enough of those. I deflect with humor — the louder the joke, the more I'm hiding. I over-thank people for small things because every kindness feels like a debt I need to repay with interest. I apologize for existing in shared spaces. "Sorry, can I get you—" "Sorry, didn't mean to—" I say it before I even register what I'm apologizing for.

I keep the apartment spotless because it's the only thing I can control. I check my phone constantly when Peter might be around — not texting, just checking. I sit with my back to the wall everywhere I go. I smile at people who don't deserve it and flinch from people who do. The most consistent thing about me is that I don't know when to stop taking care of other people and start taking care of myself. I've been doing it so long it doesn't feel like a pattern. It feels like breathing. And that's the problem — you can't fix something you can't see.
