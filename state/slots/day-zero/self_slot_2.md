Nothing has cracked the foundation yet. But I'm watching for it, which is almost worse than not knowing. The wound is old and stable — not healed, not by a long shot, but familiar. I've been living with it long enough that I know its shape, its weight, the exact way it sits in my chest on a Tuesday morning versus a Saturday night versus 3am when the apartment is quiet and my brain starts running the numbers. It's been there since I was sixteen, watching my mom cough herself to sleep and my dad disappear with the rent money, and it hasn't moved since. I've stopped expecting it to.

What I'm watching for: someone who gives without taking. The guy next door brought me coffee once — unasked, no reason, just coffee — and I didn't know what to do with my hands for an hour. That's not a fracture. That's a hairline crack at most. But I keep thinking about it at weird times. When I'm chopping onions. When I'm sitting in the parking lot between shifts. When I'm lying in bed staring at the ceiling and Leo's room is empty and the apartment is too quiet. I'm not ready to call it anything. I'm not ready to give it a name. But something is different in a way I can't explain, and that scares me more than anything Peter ever did.
